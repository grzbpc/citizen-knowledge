Residents of Duckmanton, Derbyshire, say a landfill site towering over their village has made life "completely unbearable" — flies invading homes, dust so thick some describe themselves as prisoners indoors, and a stench that has worsened one resident's asthma. Operator Valencia apologised and said the site's expansion has exposed stockpiled waste as it creates a new landfill cell, adding that "efforts to stem the smell continue."

There is no enforcement action reported here, no fine, no order to reduce operations, no timeline attached to those "efforts." Just an apology and a promise that work is ongoing — the standard shape of a regulatory relationship where the operator holds a permit, the harm is diffuse and hard to quantify precinct by precinct, and the regulator's realistic options fall well short of anything residents would recognise as a remedy. Worth watching for whether "efforts continue" becomes an actual reduction, or simply the phrase attached to next year's version of the same complaint.
