### The story

Four men, brothers Shayyan and Ehteshan Hussain, Faheem Chohan and Jawad Hussain, have been jailed for a combined 30 years over the "honour" kidnapping of a 21-year-old man in Telford, Shropshire, who had been dating the Hussain brothers' sister. The victim was dragged from his home, driven off with his legs trailing from the boot, and taken to a shipping container where he was stripped, tied to a chair and beaten, leaving him with a fractured eye socket. His father received a phone call demanding £20,000, with a threat that his son would "not be coming home" if he did not pay. The victim escaped and raised the alarm at a nearby takeaway. All four admitted a kidnap charge earlier this year. The victim told Shrewsbury crown court: "The kidnap has changed my life. I now rarely leave the house."

### The reframe

The ransom call to the victim's father is the detail worth sitting with. It was not a call to police, and there is no suggestion in this report that police were approached at any point before the abduction became a fully realised crime. The dispute, over who the sister was permitted to date, and the enforcement of the family's judgement on that question, ran entirely through private channels: surveillance, a warning delivered with a baseball bat, and finally a kidnapping with a cash demand attached. The court process that has now jailed four men only began once that private system had already run its full course and failed to remain contained.
